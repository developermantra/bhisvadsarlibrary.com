Dr. Priti Shrimal (Principal)



The Principal's Vision

As the principal of Billabong High International School Vadsar, I envision our library as a vibrant hub that fosters a love for learning and exploration among our students. Our library isn't merely a room with books but a gateway to a world of knowledge, creativity, and imagination. I see our library as a dynamic space, buzzing with intellectual curiosity and activity. It's a place where students eagerly delve into a diverse collection of books, spanning various subjects and genres, catering to both academic studies and recreational reading. The shelves are lined with not only textbooks but also literature, science, history, art, and more, catering to different interests and learning styles. I believe in leveraging technology to complement our collection—providing access to e-books, online resources, and digital databases that expand the horizons of our students beyond the physical confines of the library. A modern library should embrace innovation while cherishing the timeless joy of flipping through pages and getting lost in a captivating story. Moreover, our library isn't just about books. It's a space that encourages collaboration, critical thinking, and creativity. I envision cozy reading corners where students engage in discussions, group projects, and brainstorming sessions. It's a haven for quiet study as well as lively debate—a place where students feel empowered to voice their opinions and explore diverse perspectives. To support this vision, our library will have dedicated and knowledgeable staff—an experienced librarian passionate about cultivating a love for reading and guiding students in their quest for knowledge. They'll curate resources, organize events, and be a mentor and guide to students, fostering a lifelong love for learning. In essence, our school library embodies the spirit of exploration, discovery, and intellectual growth. It's a nurturing space where students not only acquire knowledge but also develop critical thinking skills, empathy, and a thirst for continuous learning—a foundation that will serve them well beyond their academic years."
